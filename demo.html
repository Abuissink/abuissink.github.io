I advise both local and national government on the best ways to engage youth in policy making

I am on the Orakei Youth Board, which advises the Local Board. I also sit on the wider Council Youth Advisory Panel working party helping craft the YAP selection process, and have advised the national Ministry of Social Development during the review of their youth programmes.

I am also involved in education and youth activism about world issues.

I am a High School Ambassador for UN Youth NZ and a Build Hope leader for Amnesty Aotearoa New Zealand. I run both of those clubs at Auckland Grammar School. They both educate teens on the issues and help prepare them for advocacy, as well as take part in activism on the issues.

I also code for good!

I have participated in Google Code-In in 2015 and 2016, helping open-source initiatives with their coding. Over the two years I have helped Free Open-Source Software Asia, and the Sustainable Computing Research Centre with their programmes, including DengueMap, a web application helping track mosquito breeding in an effort to stop mosquito-bourne diseases.
