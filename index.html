About

Hi, I'm Archie Buissink.

I'm on the Orakei Youth Board where I work to make the Orakei ward of Auckland a more enjoyable place for youth to work, study and live in. As part of this I consult and discuss issues with not just youth, but Orakei Local Board, wider Auckland Council and community officials.

I am passionate about giving something back to the community.

For me this takes many forms. Not just serving on the Orakei Youth Board, but also leading the Amnesty Auckland Grammar and Auckland Grammar Model United Nations clubs at my school and my position on the New Zealand Mathematical Olympiad Student Association.

I can also code!

I participated in Google Code-In 2015, where I gave up my time over summer helping open-source technology projects like Free Open-Source Software Asia (FOSSASIA) and OpenMRS, which makes open-source healthcare systems. I made this site, and I run getawesome.online, giving others awesome sites for free. It comes in handy for various other things along the way too.

My Key Principles

Guardianship | Kaitiakitanga

The natural resources we have are so amazing and so precious it is important we look after them, and ensure what we can treasure today is also able to be treasured in the future. Local government has a very important role in this preservation.

Forward Sight

Actions we make, no matter the magnitude, public sector, private sector, inside or outside our homes, have a lasting impact on us and our society. It is important that decisions are made that are beneficial in the long-term for all parties affected.

Openness

Many people, including youth, no longer believe that government represent their views. It is important to ensure that government work, even at the Youth Board level, is open and transparent to help constituents believe in its capabilities.

Liveability

Auckland is one of the most liveable cities in the world. That is a fact. I am a firm believer in community spaces such as parks and libraries, as well as community events. I have organised events such as teen movie nights at Remuera Library and see this as an important piece of Auckland's future.

UN Youth NZ

I love the United Nations and what it stands for, and I am quite heavily involved in UN Youth NZ, the youth arm of the United Nations Association New Zealand. I have attended many Model United Nations events, including the prestigious national New Zealand Model United Nations in 2016, part of which took place at NZ Parliament. I love being able to practice diplomacy and debating in events such as these.

From a leadership point of view, I have an important role in the Auckland Grammar School Model United Nations Club. I held a leadership role in the club in 2016, where I organised and ran practice debates for all so that other students could experience MUN in a no pressure environment, and helped organise school delegations to conferences. I am a candidate for Auckland Grammar's High School Ambassador to UN Youth NZ in 2017.

Amnesty Aotearoa

I am the leader of the Auckland Grammar School Amnesty group, which works with and is supported by Amnesty Aotearoa. We are a 150 member group which works to raise funds and awareness not just for Amnesty International but also other organisations such as UNICEF and NZ Red Cross. Highlights from 2016 include collecting school supplies and stationery to give to refugees resettled in NZ and hosting a quiz night to raise moneu for 'Ignite Minds not Bombs', Amnesty Aotearoa's 2016 Freedom Challenge topic.

Government Youth Access Forum

GYAF is a very new initiative of mine that is only beginning to take shape. At the moment, it is simply a peer group of youth involved in government, such as Youth MP's, Youth Councillors and even actual politicians. Hopefully this project will grow in the future, but for now it is still very much in its infancy. In the future, this would ideally provide resources for youth looking to get involved in government, and provide them with services they may not be able to themselves, like mentors, websites, etc.
