Who is Archie Buissink

Hi, I'm Archie Buissink. I'm on the Orakei Youth Board where I work to make the Orakei ward of Auckland a more enjoyable place for youth to work, study and live in. As part of this I consult and discuss issues with not just youth, but Orakei Local Board, Auckland Council and community officials.

I love Orakei and Auckland, and I am working and have worked on many exciting projects to make it a better place. Small events like youth movie nights at Remuera Library all the way up to big projects like helping connect local youth to available jobs, I do them all. You can find out about some select projects below. You can also follow my blog, Facebook or Twitter to stay up to date.

I am also involved in many other organisations outside of the Youth Board. I am on the committee of the New Zealand Mathematical Olympiad Student Association, which promotes extra-curricular maths and the International Maths Olympiad in New Zealand, a post I have held since 2015. I am an avid participator in Model United Nations conferences and UN Youth NZ, having attended many different events such as Aotearoa Youth Declaration and New Zealand Model United Nations, where I practiced useful policy-making, speaking and diplomatic skills. I really enjoy these events, and am the student leader of the Auckland Grammar Model United Nations Club.

I am the current leader of Amnesty Auckland Grammar, a position I have held since November 2015. This is one of the biggest clubs at our school, with over 150 members in 2016. What makes really cool about this club is that not only do we work with Amnesty International to raise awareness and fundraise, we also collaborate with other charities and non-profits such as Red Cross New Zealand as well as Amnesty Aotearoa.

I am also proficient in web technologies such as HTML and CSS. I built this website myself! Over the summer of 2015-2016, I was involved in a two month long project called Google Code-In where teens around the world, including me, gave their time for open-source coding initiatives. I spent the two months helping organisations such as Free Open-Source Software Asia (FOSSASIA) and OpenMRS, a global leader in open technologies and open standards in health care.
